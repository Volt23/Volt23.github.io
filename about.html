About me

I was born in Palermo in 1991 and after my diploma in Scientific studies I moved to Milan for studying Communication design at Politecnico. I've been always interested in the intersection between design and technology, and during my last year at Politecnico of Milan I started to explore the interaction design field. I got my bachelor degree in 2015 with a thesis called "Gammy Builder: an interactive prototype to enhance learning processes".

In 2016 I've decided to attend the FabAcademy at Opendot, a digital fabrication class where I learnt how to design and fabricate a circuit, how to program a firmware, how to use digital fabrication tools such as laser cutter, cnc milling, vinyl cutter and 3d printer and how to integrate those processes in artisanal practices like molding and casting, and composite. Since 2017 I've been working in dotdotdot an interaction design studio focused on tangible projects, mainly exhibition design.
